The Isle of Man TT

What is the Isle Of Man TT?

  • The Isle of Man TT is an annual motorcycle racing event.
  • It takes place on the Isle of Man, located in the Irish Sea between Great Britain and Ireland.
  • The Isle of Man TT has a rich history dating back to its inception in 1907. It is one of the oldest and most iconic motorcycle road racing events in the world.
  • It is considered one of the most prestigious and dangerous motorcycle road racing events in the world.

The Track.

  • The TT course or known as The Snaefell Mountain Course is approximately 37.73 miles (60.72 kilometers) long. It is known as the longest and most demanding road racing circuit in the world.
  • Riders average speed a lap is 128MPH
  • The track consists of 217 Turns which some are took at full speed (200MPH)
  • The TT features a wide range of race catagories such as: Superbike, Supersport, Superstock, and Sidecar races.
  • Lap times of the TT on average are about 17 minutes (it would take you just over an hour in your car on a normal day driving the circuit)
  • The track consists of public roads that are temporarily closed for racing during the event. The roads wind through towns, villages, and the beautiful countryside, offering a mix of narrow lanes, fast straights, and challenging corners.
